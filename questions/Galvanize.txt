**Question:**

Which of the following sentences uses the word "galvanize" correctly?

A) The artist's latest piece was meant to galvanize the audience into contemplating social issues.

B) The tranquil scene at the park was so calming that it galvanized him into a deep sleep.

C) Despite the storm, the small boat remained galvanized and unmoving on the water.

D) Her approach to work is to galvanize any challenges until they become too easy to solve.

**Correct Answer: A)** The artist's latest piece was meant to galvanize the audience into contemplating social issues.

---

In this context, "galvanize" is used correctly to mean stimulating or inciting the audience into awareness or activity.
